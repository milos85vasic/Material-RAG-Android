JCenter service update
Stay organized with collections Save and categorize content based on your preferences.

JFrog, the company that maintains the JCenter artifact repository used by many Android projects, made JCenter a read-only repository on March 31, 2021. According to the announcement, JCenter will allow downloads of existing artifacts indefinitely.

Developers who publish artifacts on JCenter should migrate their packages to a new host, such as Maven Central.

Developers who use dependencies from JCenter must find the new location of updated versions of those dependencies.

Warning: Don't include the jcenter() repository in your build config to avoid getting outdated dependencies.
